The Problem

The Sport
Takes
Everything.
Gives Back
Nothing.

A player trains for years. Attends every trial. Represents their school, their county, their country in youth tournaments. Their parents spend money they don't have. Coaches invest hours they'll never recover.

Then the academy releases them. Or the national setup doesn't call. Or they age out — and the industry treats this as natural, even inevitable. It isn't. It's a systems failure with a fixable cost.

The global football industry employs millions of people who never played professionally. Coaches, analysts, kit designers, fitness scientists, barbers, physiotherapists, groundskeepers, referees, stewards, data scouts. These roles exist everywhere. They just don't exist yet as an organized pathway out of the rejection funnel.

For nations outside the top half of the FIFA rankings — for Kenya, for Uganda, for those who did not make the World Cup — this is a talent retention crisis hiding in plain sight.

"The best 18-year-old in Chapa Dimba who didn't make it to Harambee Stars under-20 is not a failure. They're an unclaimed asset."

What We
Actually Do

01 — Intake
Identify and Verify

We identify 18-year-old players at the 85th percentile and above, using verified tournament records, academy assessments, and coach recommendations. We work through Chapa Dimba, Cheza Dimba, and county FA networks. Every enrolled player has a verified Index — a digital player record showing what they did and where they played.

Chapa Dimba Academy records Coach referrals
02 — Develop
Build the Professional

Players enrol in a twelve-month residential and blended programme. They choose a primary career track, receive 300+ hours of structured instruction, and complete supervised placements with partner clubs and organizations. Development is documented weekly. Coaches, mentors, and industry practitioners lead sessions — many of them reclaimed talent themselves.

12 months 300+ hours Track placement
03 — Place
Deploy into the Industry

Graduates are placed directly with clubs, academies, national associations, brands, and football businesses. The programme maintains an active talent market — partners come to us because our graduates understand the game from the inside. Placement is a measured outcome and a revenue mechanism; we charge placement fees to hiring organizations.

Club placements Association roles Industry partners

The Top 1% — Elite Pathway Re-Entry
For the Exceptional Few, the Database Opens a Different Door.

Within every cohort, a small number of players will produce Index data that places them unambiguously in a different category. Not just good programme graduates — demonstrably elite. Heart rate, movement data, tactical metrics, session records, coach assessments: all verified, all timestamped, all auditable.

For these players, the FRP becomes something different. Not a career transition — a second chance at the first career. The data makes the argument the player could not make alone. A trial is a performance on one day. An Index is a performance over 365 days. No scout misses that.

Live Now 📊
Index Athlete
The Verified Player Record

The foundation layer. Every player in the FRP has a digital Index — a verifiable, structured record of their football history, programme progress, track completion, and placement status. Built on coach verification, tournament records, and weekly session logs. Clubs, FAs, and employers query it. Players own it.

  • Verified tournament and academy history
  • Track progress documented weekly
  • Coach-signed session records
  • Placement-ready at graduation
  • FA and club access via annual licence
  • Player-controlled visibility settings
Phase 03 Rollout 📷
Index Eyesight
AI-Enabled Training Intelligence

Fixed cameras installed in Isikuti Soka training facilities and partner grounds. AI-processed video generates automatic movement tracking, positional heat maps, and session metrics — without requiring a human analyst present. Data feeds directly into the player's Index. Nothing is manual. Everything is timestamped and auditable.

  • Fixed multi-angle cameras, no operator required
  • Automatic player tagging via biometric profile
  • Sprint distance, acceleration, work rate per session
  • Positional heat maps per player and team
  • Auto-push to Index Athlete record
  • Clip library for coach and player review
Long Game ⌚
Index Watches
Wearable Performance Hardware

A wearable designed for East African training conditions and body types — measuring heart rate, recovery, sleep, physical exertion, and readiness. Not a consumer product adapted for sport. A tool built from FRP training data, for players who train in Nairobi's heat, Eldoret's altitude, Kisumu's humidity. Data from the watch syncs with Index Eyesight and feeds the player's full biometric profile.

  • Heart rate, HRV, and recovery scoring
  • Training load and readiness index per session
  • Sleep quality and rest period tracking
  • GPS movement in outdoor training environments
  • Syncs with Index Eyesight and Index Athlete
  • Designed and manufactured from FRP training data
